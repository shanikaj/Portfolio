. Landmarks Around The World
Mar 2020 - Present

During my co-op at ecobee, I was responsible for the re-design of two short adapters used internally to flash firmware onto an ecobee3 and an ecobee3 lite Smart Thermostat. Using a honeycomb structure along with a bee shaped backplate designed in SolidWorks, I was able to yield an overall 74% price reduction. I also improved upon the corresponding short adapter PCB's in KiCad by adding a ground plane and relocating components to optimize routing.

As a member of the Sensors squad, I also executed an in-depth investigation into SmartSensor performance based on RF signals, with the results being used to develop a design standard for future projects. Furthermore, I worked on the automation infrastructure in Python for end to end verification of SmartSensor features.

My co-op at Teledyne DALSA was the first to truly make me feel like a Mechatronics Engineer: I was able to work on a variety of projects ranging from mechanical to software, and was given full autonomy over them to lead them to completion. My greatest accomplishment was the designed of an assembly fixture to reform shielding of inter-oral detector, thereby optimizing workflows in support of Lean Six Sigma initiatives and saving operators 3 hours a week as a result!

The majority of my time was spent dedicated towards solving x-ray production issues by designing 3D printed tools and jigs that enhanced the product build accuracy and improved technician work ergonomics. Furthermore, I liaised with senior engineers to draft mechanical drawings of jigs using GD&T. In terms of software projects, I created a barcode scanner verification program in HTML/CSS/JS to improve process control. I also created a PowerShell script to automatically retrieve a specific ID tag from an inter-oral detector and input them into a spreadsheet.
